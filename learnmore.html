EARTHQUAKE

OVERCOME

*Ensure Safety First: Prioritize safety immediately after the earthquake by checking for injuries and securing your surroundings. Follow safety protocols such as "Drop, Cover, and Hold On" during the quake and evacuate damaged buildings if necessary.

*Communicate and Reconnect: Use available communication channels to inform loved ones of your safety and whereabouts. Reconnect with family, friends, and neighbors to provide support and gather information about the situation.

*Prepare for Future Events: Learn from the earthquake experience and take proactive measures to enhance preparedness for future disasters. Update emergency plans, stock emergency supplies, and reinforce buildings against seismic hazards.

*Build Resilience: Work with local authorities and community organizations to build resilience against future earthquakes and other natural hazards. Support initiatives for infrastructure improvements, disaster risk reduction, and public education.

*Stay Positive and Adaptive: Overcoming the impact of an earthquake can be a challenging process, but maintaining a positive attitude and being adaptive to change can help in moving forward. Focus on rebuilding and recovery efforts while drawing strength from the support of others.

FLOOD

OVERCOME

*Ensure Safety First: Before anything else, prioritize safety. Ensure that it's safe to return to your home or area affected by the flood. Avoid entering floodwaters, as they may be contaminated and pose health risks.

*Assess the Damage: Once it's safe to do so, assess the extent of the damage to your property. Take photographs or videos for insurance claims and documentation purposes. Identify items that are salvageable and those that need to be discarded.

*Seek Emotional Support: Dealing with the aftermath of a flood can be emotionally challenging. Seek support from friends, family, or counseling services to cope with stress, anxiety, and trauma associated with the disaster.

HURRICANE

OVERCOME

*Prepare an emergency kit with essential supplies such as water, non-perishable food, medications, flashlights, batteries, and important documents.

*Secure your home by boarding up windows, reinforcing doors, and securing outdoor objects that could become projectiles in high winds

*Evacuate if authorities recommend or mandate it, especially if you live in a low-lying area prone to flooding or storm surge.

*Have a communication plan in place with family and friends, including a designated meeting point in case you get separated.

*Keep your car fueled and ready to go in case you need to evacuate quickly.

TORNADO

OVERCOME

*Stay Safe: Prioritize your safety and the safety of others. Follow instructions from local authorities and avoid entering damaged buildings until they have been declared safe.

*Seek Support: Lean on friends, family, and community resources for emotional support and practical assistance. You're not alone in this recovery process.

*Assess Damage: Evaluate the extent of the damage to your property and possessions. Take inventory of what has been lost or damaged, and document everything for insurance claims.

DROUGHT

OVERCOME

*Diversification of water sources: Invest in alternative water sources such as rainwater harvesting, desalination, and wastewater recycling to augment water supply during dry periods.

*Drought-resistant infrastructure: Develop infrastructure resilient to drought, including water-efficient irrigation systems, leak detection technologies, and water storage facilities.

*Climate change adaptation: Address the underlying causes of drought by implementing measures to mitigate climate change and adapt to its impacts.

*Community engagement: Foster community involvement in water management through education, participation in water conservation programs, and support for local initiatives.

LANDSLIDE

OVERCOME

*Prevention and Mitigation: Implement slope stabilization measures such as retaining walls, terracing, and vegetation management.

*Early Warning Systems:Deploy monitoring technologies such as inclinometers, ground-penetrating radar, and satellite imagery to detect slope movement.

*Emergency Response: Develop emergency response plans that outline roles and responsibilities for government agencies, emergency services, and community organizations.

*Infrastructure Resilience:Design infrastructure such as roads, bridges, and buildings to withstand landslide forces and minimize damage.

*Long-Term Planning and Adaptation:Integrate landslide risk reduction into broader disaster risk management and climate adaptation strategies.

CYCLONES

OVERCOME

*Ensure Safety: Prioritize safety first. Make sure you and your loved ones are out of harm's way and have access to necessary medical care if needed.

* Assess Damage: Evaluate the extent of damage to your property and belongings. Document everything thoroughly for insurance purposes.

*Seek Assistance: Reach out to local authorities, relief agencies, and community organizations for support. They can provide resources, information, and assistance with recovery efforts.

*Clean Up and Repair: Begin cleaning up debris and making necessary repairs to your home or property. Work with professionals if needed, especially for structural repairs.

STORM

OVERCOME

*Stay positive: Maintaining a positive attitude can help you cope with the challenges ahead.

*Seek support: Lean on friends, family, or community resources for emotional and practical support.

* Focus on safety: Prioritize safety by addressing any immediate hazards and following instructions from authorities.

*Practice self-care: Take care of your physical and mental health by getting enough rest, eating well, and engaging in activities that bring you comfort.

*Be patient: Recovery takes time, so be patient with yourself and others as you navigate the process.

THUDERSTORM

OVERCOME

*Stay Calm:Maintain a calm demeanor to think clearly and make rational decisions.

*Seek Shelter: Move to a sturdy building or vehicle to seek shelter from lightning, heavy rain, and strong winds.

* Avoid Electrical Appliances: Unplug electrical appliances to prevent damage from power surges or lightning strikes.

*Stay Informed: Listen to weather updates on a battery-powered radio or smartphone to stay informed about the storm's progress and any emergency instructions.

*Stay Indoors: Remain indoors until the storm passes and it's safe to venture outside.

OVERCOME

* Physical Recovery:Seek immediate medical attention for any injuries sustained during the explosion.

*Emotional Recovery: Seek support from friends, family, or mental health professionals to process the emotional trauma of the explosion.

*Community Support:Lean on the support of your community and local resources for assistance with recovery efforts.

FORESTFIRE

OVERCOME

*Safety First: Ensure the safety of yourself and others before anything else. Follow the guidance of local authorities and emergency services.

*Assess Damage: Evaluate the extent of the damage to your property, infrastructure, and the surrounding environment. Take inventory of what has been lost and what can be salvaged.

*Seek Support: Reach out to friends, family, neighbors, and community organizations for emotional support and practical assistance. You're not alone in this, and there are often resources available to help you through the recovery process.

*Work with Authorities: Cooperate with local authorities, disaster relief organizations, and insurance companies to access resources and assistance programs available for recovery.

*Rebuild and Replant: Begin the process of rebuilding your home or property, and consider participating in reforestation efforts to restore the ecosystem. Planting native vegetation can help prevent erosion and promote habitat recovery.

*Address Emotional Needs: Recognize and address any emotional trauma or stress resulting from the forest fire. Seek counseling or support groups if needed.

AVALANCHE

OVERCOME

*Swim and Fight: If you're buried, try to stay on the surface by "swimming" and make swimming motions. Try to stay on top of the snow as it settles

*Try to Escape: Move to the side or try to grab onto a tree if you're still on the surface.

*Swim and Fight: If you're buried, try to stay on the surface by "swimming" and make swimming motions. Try to stay on top of the snow as it settles.

*Create an Air Pocket: Cover your mouth and nose with one arm to create an air pocket as the snow settles. This can give you more time to breathe.

*Stay Calm: Conserve your energy and try to remain calm. Panic uses up oxygen and can cloud judgment.

*Signal for Help: If you have a transceiver, activate it to help rescuers locate you. If you don't have one, try to make noise or use anything you have to signal your location.

*Create Space: Make room for movement by pushing snow away from your face and body. Try to create an airspace around your head.

*Wait for Rescue: Once the avalanche stops, stay put. Rescuers will be using transceivers and probes to search for buried victims. Moving around can make it harder for them to find you.
