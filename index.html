EARTHQUAKE

PRE-PLANNING:

*Prepare an Emergency Kit: Assemble an emergency kit containing essentials such as water, non-perishable food, first aid supplies, flashlight, batteries, portable radio, and any necessary medications. Store these in an easily accessible location.

*Create an Emergency Plan: Develop a family emergency plan detailing how to communicate and where to meet in case you're separated during an earthquake. Practice this plan regularly with all family members.

*Secure Heavy Items: Secure heavy furniture, appliances, and objects to walls or floors using straps, braces, or adhesives. This prevents them from falling during an earthquake.

*Protect Your Home: Secure heavy items in your home like bookcases, refrigerators, water heaters, televisions and objects that hang on walls. Store heavy and breakable objects on low shelves

POST-PLANNING:

*Check for Injuries and Damage: Immediately check yourself and others for injuries. If safe to do so, check for damages to your surroundings, including gas leaks, fallen power lines, and structural damage.

*Evacuate if Necessary: If your home or building is severely damaged, evacuate immediately. Use stairs rather than elevators, and watch out for hazards such as debris and broken glass.

*Communicate with Others: Use text messages or social media to communicate with family and friends, as phone lines may be overwhelmed. Let someone know you are safe if possible.

*Stay Informed: Stay informed about the situation through radio broadcasts, social media updates, or emergency alerts. Follow any instructions provided by authorities to ensure your safety.

*Document Damage: Document any damage to your property by taking photos or videos. This will be helpful for insurance claims

FLOOD

PRE-PLANNING:

*Stay Informed: Keep track of weather updates, especially if you live in flood-prone areas. Listen to local authorities and follow their instructions.

*Emergency Kit: Prepare an emergency kit that includes essential items such as non-perishable food, water, medications, flashlights, batteries, a first aid kit, and important documents

*Flood Insurance: Consider purchasing flood insurance if you live in a flood-prone area. Standard homeowner's insurance typically does not cover flood damage.

*Waterproofing: Seal basement walls and windows with waterproofing compounds. Install sump pumps and consider installing check valves to prevent sewage backup.

*Emergency Contacts: Keep a list of emergency contacts handy, including local authorities, utilities, and emergency services.

POST-PLANNING:

*Sanitation: Disinfect surfaces and items that came into contact with floodwater to prevent the spread of bacteria and mold.

*Safety First: Avoid walking or driving through floodwaters, as they may be contaminated or hiding hazards. Be cautious of downed power lines and unstable buildings.

*Assess Damage: After it's safe to return, assess the damage to your property. Take photographs or videos for insurance claims.

*Emotional Support: Floods can be traumatic events. Seek support from friends, family, or counseling services if needed.

>*Prevent Future Damage: Take steps to prevent future floods, such as installing flood barriers, elevating utilities, and reinforcing vulnerable areas of your home.

HURRICANE

PRE-PLANNING:

*Stay Informed: Monitor weather updates and warnings from reliable sources like the National Hurricane Center or your local meteorological service.

*Create a Plan: Develop an evacuation plan with your family. Determine evacuation routes, designate meeting points, and establish communication methods.

*Secure Your Home: Reinforce windows and doors with storm shutters or plywood. Trim trees and shrubs, and clear gutters to minimize debris. Anchor or bring in outdoor furniture, decorations, and objects that could become projectiles.

*Fuel and Transportation: Fill up your vehicles with gas ahead of time. Consider having alternative transportation arrangements if necessary.

POST-PLANNING:

*Stay in Touch: Keep in contact with friends, family, and neighbors to share updates and support one another.

*Assess Damage: Once it's safe to do so, assess the damage to your property. Be cautious of hazards like downed power lines, standing water, and unstable structures.

*Check for Injuries: Attend to any injuries or medical emergencies promptly. Contact emergency services if necessary.

*Reach Out for Help: Seek assistance from local authorities, relief organizations, or community groups if you need support with shelter, food, or supplies.

*Document Damage: Take photos or videos of any damage to your property for insurance purposes.

TORNADO

PRE-PLANNING:

*Have a Plan: Create a tornado emergency plan for your family or household, including designated safe areas within your home.

*Prepare an Emergency Kit: Stock up on essentials like water, non-perishable food, flashlights, batteries, a first aid kit, and any necessary medications.

*Practice Drills: Regularly conduct tornado drills with your family or household members so everyone knows what to do in case of an emergency.

*Stay Safe: Prioritize your safety and the safety of others. Follow instructions from local authorities and avoid entering damaged buildings until they have been declared safe.

POST-PLANNING:

*Check for Injuries: Attend to any injuries and seek medical attention if necessary. Use caution when moving around debris.

*Assess Damage: Survey your property for damage, but only enter heavily damaged buildings if it's safe to do so.

*Listen for Updates: Stay tuned to local news and authorities for updates on the situation, including information on shelters, road closures, and potential hazards.

*Contact Loved Ones: Let friends and family know you're safe, and check on the well-being of others if possible.

*Avoid Hazards: Be cautious of downed power lines, gas leaks, and unstable structures. Stay away from flooded areas as well.

LANDSLIDE

PRE-PLANNING:

*Risk assessment: Identify areas prone to landslides through geological surveys and mapping

*Land use planning: Implement zoning regulations to restrict development in high-risk landslide areas and encourage sustainable land use practices.

*Vegetation management: Maintain or restore natural vegetation, which helps stabilize soil and reduce the risk of landslides.

*Early warning systems: Establish monitoring systems to detect signs of slope instability and provide timely warnings to at-risk communities.

*Emergency preparedness: Develop evacuation plans and educate residents on safety measures to take in the event of a landslide.

POST-PLANNING:

*Assessment: Evaluate the extent of damage and assess the risk of further landslides in affected areas.

*Search and rescue: Conduct search and rescue operations to locate and assist any individuals trapped or injured by the landslide.

* Infrastructure repair: Repair damaged roads, utilities, and other infrastructure to restore essential services to affected communities.

*Rehabilitation: Implement measures to stabilize slopes, prevent erosion, and restore natural vegetation in landslide-prone areas.

*Risk reduction: Implement long-term measures to reduce the risk of future landslides, such as slope reinforcement, drainage improvements, and land use planning.

*Community support: Provide assistance and support to affected communities, including temporary housing, counseling, and access to healthcare services.

EXPLOSION

PRE-PLANNING:

*Safety Measures: Ensure all necessary safety precautions are in place, such as wearing appropriate protective gear, establishing a safe distance from the explosion site, and securing the area.

*Evacuation: Evacuate people from the vicinity of the explosion to a safe distance, following established evacuation procedures.

*Secure Utilities: Shut off gas, electricity, and other utilities to minimize the risk of secondary hazards like fire or electrocution.

*Communications: Inform relevant authorities and emergency services about the impending explosion, providing necessary details to coordinate response efforts.

POST-PLANNING:

*Safety Assessment:Assess the situation to determine if it's safe to approach the explosion site, considering risks such as structural damage, fires, and hazardous materials.

*Search and Rescue: Conduct search and rescue operations to locate and assist any individuals who may be injured or trapped in the aftermath of the explosion.

*Medical Assistance:*Provide medical assistance to injured individuals, prioritizing those with life-threatening injuries and coordinating with emergency medical services.

*Investigation: Investigate the cause of the explosion to determine any contributing factors, identify responsible parties, and prevent future incidents.Cleanup and Recovery: Begin cleanup efforts to remove debris, restore infrastructure, and support the affected community in recovering from the aftermath of the explosion.

AVALANCHE

PRE-PLANNING:

*Check Avalanche Conditions: Monitor weather and avalanche forecasts to assess the risk level.

*Plan Your Route: Choose safe terrain and avoid avalanche-prone areas

*Equip Yourself: Carry avalanche safety gear including a transceiver, shovel, and probe.

*Inform Others: Share your itinerary and expected return time with someone reliable.

*Travel in Groups: Whenever possible, travel with partners and stay within visual and auditory contact

POST-PLANNING:

*Check for Danger: Ensure that no further avalanches or hazards are imminent.

*Assess Injuries: Attend to any injured individuals and provide first aid if necessary.

*Search and Rescue: Use avalanche transceivers, probes, and shovels to search for buried victims.

*Support Survivors: Provide emotional support to survivors and coordinate evacuation or medical assistance as needed

*Debrief and Learn: After the event, conduct a debriefing session to analyze what happened and learn from the experience to improve future safety measures.

*Public Awareness: Communicate lessons learned and updates to the evacuation plan to raise awareness and improve community preparedness for future disasters.

*Hazard Mapping: Identify areas prone to permafrost-related hazards such as ground subsidence, landslides, and infrastructure damage.

*Infrastructure Design: Design infrastructure projects with permafrost considerations, such as pile foundations and thermal insulation, to minimize permafrost thaw impacts.

*Community Awareness: Educate communities living in permafrost regions about the risks associated with permafrost degradation and methods to mitigate these risks.

*Research and Innovation: Invest in research and development to better understand permafrost dynamics and develop innovative solutions for mitigating permafrost-related hazards.

POST-PLANNING:

*Damage Assessment: Assess the extent of damage to infrastructure, ecosystems, and communities caused by permafrost thaw-related hazards.

*Infrastructure Repair and Reinforcement: Repair damaged infrastructure and implement measures to reinforce structures against future permafrost-related hazards.

*Environmental Remediation: Restore affected ecosystems and mitigate environmental impacts resulting from permafrost degradation, such as soil erosion and habitat loss.

*Community Resilience Building: Provide support and resources to help communities adapt to changing permafrost conditions and reduce vulnerability to future disasters.
